WUROV (Underwater Vehicle)

Building an underwater vehicle that can swim in open water to improve the exploration of the deep sea. This project has been an ongoing project of IEEE chapter at Wentworth for 5+ years. I am currently running the team and setting up roles for Elecrical, Mechancial, and Programming teams. The robot needs to be able to detect objects underwater through image recognition algorithms as well as using radar to reach certain positions insdie a closed pool.

Pico-Satellite Wentworth

Launching a 5cm pico-satellite to take a picture of the earth in low Earth orbit. I am currently the Energy and Power Systems (EPS) leader who is in charge of the power harvesting electronics and the battery charging circuits. The group plans to present our work on the EPS at the 2023 ASEE Aerospace Conference in Maryland.

Synchronized Drone Show

Using python camera tracking systems to synchronize 100 to 1000 drones at Wentworth Institute of Technology. We have split this project into two stages. The first stage includes the tracking of drones in open areas. We plan to do this by laying out a 3D array where each element will be a location a drone can go to. The second stage of the project will be to add multiple drones to the matrix and create different animations and logos without collision.

WiFi Controlled RC Car

Learned about h-bride controllers to drive four dc motors simultaneously from a WiFi connection to a raspbarry pi.

Keyboard

Designed my own PCB containing sixteen key-switches and 2 rotary encoders. Programmed an Atmega32u4 to be a Human Interface Device(HID) using the key presses and encoder values.

Power Supply

Built a 12V power supply that plugs into a 115 VAC power supply. The unit was than used to power a control drive which was used to test the endurance of small dc motors.

Spider Robot

Exploring into the biomechancis of organisms through building a spider with 12 joints. Three joints make up each leg which I had to program an algorithm to move correctly. This project is something I look to continue in the future as I was not fully successful with my design.
